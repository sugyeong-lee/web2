  1. Bibimbap

    Bibimbap is a Korean rice dish. It is a food that is mixed with various seasonings such as meat and vegetables in rice, and is also called bibimbap, ritual rice, antique, and stir. In the past, the court called it Bibim. It is considered one of the national foods of Korea.

    recipe

    1. Open the refrigerator and take out side dishes including vegetables.
    2. Spoon the rice and put it in a large bowl.
    3. Place all the side dishes taken out on top of the rice bowl.
    4. Add a spoonful of red pepper paste (soy sauce, soybean paste, or ssamjang if you don't like spicy food) and a spoonful of sesame oil. (A spoonful of red pepper paste without a high salary.) You may need to add a little more sauce other than red pepper paste.)
    5. Stir-fried red pepper paste with red pepper paste while stir-frying beef enhances the taste of bibimbap, so please refer to it.
    6. Add a fried egg according to your preference.
    7. Rub it hard. The birth of delicious bibimbap.

  2. Pork belly

    It refers to the part of the belly of a pig attached near the ribs of a pig. It is called pork belly because the fat looks overlapped in three layers. Looking at the appearance, it is in the order of fat-fleshed meat-fleshed meat-fleshed meat-fleshed meat. It is to eat a back wall consisting of three layers of muscles, such as the outside of the belly, the belly root, and the belly root.

    In Korea, it is the most preferred pork part to the extent that it is considered the number one preference menu at restaurants or company dinners. Domestic production alone is tight enough to meet demand and imports a lot, but as of 2020, the proportion of pork belly (128,000 tons) in pork imports is more than twice that of second-largest wooden core (54,000 tons). It is usually made of grilled pork belly and eaten wrapped in sesame leaves or lettuce.

    recipe

    3. Put meat in sauce and eat it wrapped in lettuce.

  3. Japchae

    Japchae is a dish mixed with boiled glass noodles in stir-fried meat, mushrooms, and vegetables. In general, it refers to a Korean food that is seasoned with soy sauce after boiling sweet potato starch noodles in boiling water and draining them, stir-frying them with sliced beef, carrots, spinach, mushrooms, and onions. Japchae is often decorated with sesame seeds on top.

    There are times when japchae is ordered as a main dish, but in Korean cuisine, it is mainly eaten as a side dish. In addition, when eaten like rice with rice covered with rice, it is called japchae rice, and at this time, it is also served with jajang sauce.

    recipe

    1. Soak 'mangmyeon' in water.
    2. Marinate the meat with 2 spoons of cooking wine, 2 spoons of soy sauce, 1 spoon of sesame oil, 1/2 spoon of garlic, and a little pepper.
    3. Trim spinach, carrots, onions, paprika and mushrooms and fry in a frying pan.
    4. Stir-fry pork.
    5. Boil the glass noodles.
    6. Put noodles in a frying pan and stir-fry 2 spoons of cooking oil, 6 spoons of soy sauce, 2 spoons of sugar, 1/2 spoon of minced garlic, and 1 spoon of sesame oil.
    7. Add stir-fried vegetables and stir-fry again.
    8. Taste and make up for the shortcomings.
